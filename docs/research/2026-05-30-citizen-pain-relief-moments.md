## TL;DR
- **Five pain clusters dominate** the German Behörden experience, all quantified or first-person-attested: (1) Termin-Vergabe-Chaos + black-box waiting ("refresh 20×/day", weeks-to-months, no tracking tool); (2) Behördendeutsch (only **4 %** find it understandable; **48 %** struggle; **75 %** feel overwhelmed; only **1 in 5** understands a letter first read); (3) Mehrfacheingabe / no Once-Only; (4) Fristen-Angst with real money loss (Elterngeld lost forever after 3 months; Kindergeld capped at 6 retroactive months); (5) Zuständigkeits-Pingpong + "is anyone even working on my case?".[^1][^2][^3][^4][^5]
- **The mood is measured and bleak-proof**: only **12 %** of Germans say the state makes their life easier; **59 %** find Behördenkontakt "sehr anstrengend"; only **16 %** think administration works as efficiently as private firms; state trust fell to **33 %** (from 38 % in 2022).[^6][^7]
- **The demo already lands 3 of the 5** (Umzug autopilot → Mehrfacheingabe + cascade; Brief-Erklärer → Behördendeutsch; Stammdaten SSoT → re-typing). The **"it did it FOR me" punch is weakest or missing** on: appointment-getting **with live progress-tracking**, **proactive Fristen-rescue** (catching a deadline before money is lost), and the **black-box "is anyone working on this?"** anxiety.
- **Relief is real and copyable**: where a digital path works end-to-end, satisfaction is high — eWA users who completed it online report **83 %** satisfaction (eGov Monitor 2025).[^8] Denmark/Estonia/Singapore show the cascade + status-tracker + proactive-nudge patterns already delight at scale.
- **The single sharpest unmet emotional beat**: the **value-receipt + live status** — citizens rage that they cannot *see* progress ("no tracking tool", case-worker reassignment sends you "to the end of the queue"). The autopilot already produces a receipt; extending that receipt-feeling to *every* slow process (a "we are watching your case for you" surface) is the highest-leverage emotional add.

## Findings

### 1. Termin-Vergabe-Chaos + the black-box wait (universally hated, partly improving)
First-person accounts are visceral. Foreign residents describe refreshing the appointment site **"20 times a day"**, browsers crashing from constant refresh, appointments **"weeks away"**, and a registration approval that took **"more than five months"** in a rural district.[^1][^4] At the Ausländerbehörde one resident **"stood seven hours in line"** only to then wait for a verification code **sent by physical mail**.[^5] A recurring, specific complaint is the **absence of a tracking tool**: Shaik Abdul Cader (Munich) criticised the lack of any way "to show applicants where they are in the process", and a case-worker change can send an application **"to the end of the queue"**.[^4][^5]

The Berlin picture is improving — for the first time, in **March 2026** Berlin's Bürgerämter met every appointment request within 14 days (up from 48.5 % in March 2023 → 74.0 % in March 2025), and >460 services are now doable digitally, Wohnsitzanmeldung even without a Termin.[^9] But waits still **swing from a few days to three months**, and the improvement is Berlin-specific — the national pain persists.[^9]

**Inverse relief moment for the demo**: *"You don't hunt for a slot and you're never in the dark."* The system books the right Termin for you at the right Behörde, and gives a **live, honest status** ("eingegangen → in Bearbeitung → Sachbearbeiter:in zugewiesen → entschieden") with an estimated wait — the thing every account says is missing.

### 2. Behördendeutsch — letters nobody can read (strongly attested)
A 2024 survey of 2,039 adults (Qualtrics × WORTLIGA) found only **4 %** find Behördensprache understandable; **48 %** have problems understanding official letters; **69 %** must read them several times; **75 %** feel overwhelmed; only **1 in 5** understands a letter on first read.[^2][^3] The linguistic causes are concrete and well-documented: long nested Schachtelsätze, Passiv-overuse, Nominalstil (noun chains), and unnecessary legal jargon.[^10][^3] Multiple Normenkontrollräte (e.g. BW) commissioned the Leibniz-Institut für Deutsche Sprache to produce plain-language handreichungen — i.e. the *state itself* concedes the problem.[^11]

**Demo state**: **addressed (strong)** by the Posteingang Brief-Erklärer. This is a confirmed bullseye — keep it prominent. Possible amplification: show the *before/after* (a real Schachtelsatz next to its plain-language + action). The 4 %/48 %/75 % numbers are quotable proof for the Loom.

### 3. Mehrfacheingabe / no Once-Only (structural, legally named)
Citizens re-enter the same data to every Behörde because registers don't share. The Once-Only-Prinzip — submit data "nur ein einziges Mal", authorities reuse it — is the named EU/DE remedy (RegMoG 2021, Steuer-ID as Ordnungsmerkmal, Datenschutzcockpit), but rollout is partial.[^12] Expats specifically rage about **"repetitive document requests"** and re-submitting on paper.[^5][^4]

**Demo state**: **addressed (strong)** by Stammdaten SSoT + Umzug cascade ("never type your address twice"). Bullseye. Amplification: make the *absence* of re-typing visible — a counter ("38 Felder, die Sie NICHT ausfüllen mussten") turns a non-event into a felt win.

### 4. Fristen-Angst with real, irreversible money loss (under-exploited emotional goldmine)
This is the most underused beat and it maps directly onto Anna's persona. **Elterngeld is paid retroactively for at most the last 3 Lebensmonate** before the application month — miss it and **those months are gone forever** (apply in month 5 → lose 2 months).[^13] **Kindergeld** is now capped at **6 months** retroactive.[^13] Anna's documented pain ("missed the first 3 months' retroactively-claimable Kindergeld window") is therefore not a soft inconvenience — it is **hundreds of euros lost permanently** because nobody told her the clock started at birth.

**Demo state**: **weak / mostly missing.** Dashboard "Fristen" shows deadlines you already know about. The far stronger moment is **proactive rescue**: the system detects an *unclaimed entitlement with a closing window* and acts before money is lost. "Mia wurde geboren → wir haben Ihren Elterngeld-Antrag vorbereitet; Frist in 47 Tagen; bestätigen genügt." This is "it did it FOR me" at its most visceral, because it prevents an irreversible loss the citizen didn't even know to fear.

### 5. Zuständigkeits-Pingpong + black-box "is anyone working on this?" (named, legally grounded)
Citizens get bounced between offices that each deny competence; §25 VwVfG already obliges authorities to advise on rights/duties and §16 VwVfG governs onward-referral, yet in practice people are sent "from one office to another".[^14] Combined with no status visibility (§1) and case-worker resets (§1), the emotional residue is **helplessness** — The Local's interviewees describe "anxiety, stress, and helplessness".[^5]

**Demo state**: **mostly missing.** The Umzug autopilot dissolves *one* instance of pingpong (it routes to the right Behörden for you), but there is no generalised "we figured out who is responsible and we're talking to them for you" surface, and no honest live status. This is the relief inverse of pain #1 and #5 combined.

### 6. The relief side — what genuinely delights (copyable)
Relief in German admin is real wherever a path works end-to-end. Where citizens completed eWA online, **83 %** were satisfied (eGov Monitor 2025) — the single best in-country proof that the cascade model lands emotionally.[^8] Sachsen reports eWA used "ohne Wartezeiten und Behördengänge", explicitly framed as relief for citizens and administration.[^15] International precedent (already in `docs/research/2026-05-08-umzug-autopilot.md`): Denmark borger.dk Flytning (5-day cascade to PostNord/Krankenkarte/Hausarzt), Estonia X-Road once-only, Singapore MyInfo address propagation, UK "Tell Us Once" (births/deaths). The recurring delight ingredients: **(a) one action → many parties; (b) visible confirmations; (c) proactive nudge before a deadline; (d) plain language.**

## Implications for our demo
- **Keep the three confirmed bullseyes loud** (Umzug cascade, Brief-Erklärer, SSoT) — and *quantify* them on screen with the verified stats (4 % understand Behördendeutsch; 83 % eWA satisfaction; "X Felder nicht ausgefüllt"). Proof numbers make the wow defensible to Behörden insiders.
- **Add a "live, honest status + value-receipt for everything slow"** surface (generalise the autopilot receipt). The most-repeated first-person complaint is *no tracking tool* and *being sent to the end of the queue*. A truthful status ladder ("Sachbearbeiter:in zugewiesen", est. wait, "wir warten für Sie") is a low-cost, high-relief moment that no German portal credibly offers today. Candidate spine extension or strong supporting surface.
- **Build a proactive Fristen-rescue moment** around Anna/Familie Schmidt: detect an entitlement whose retroactive window is closing (Elterngeld 3 Mo / Kindergeld 6 Mo), pre-fill it, surface "Frist in N Tagen — bestätigen genügt". This is the strongest *missing* "it did it FOR me" beat because it prevents irreversible money loss the citizen didn't know about. Domain-expert must validate the exact §§ (BEEG for Elterngeld retroactivity; §66 EStG for Kindergeld 6-month rule).
- **Frame Termin-getting as "we got you the slot"** not "here's a calendar to hunt" — and pair it with the status ladder. The Berlin 14-day-success story (March 2026) is a credibility prop that the bar is rising, not fantasy.
